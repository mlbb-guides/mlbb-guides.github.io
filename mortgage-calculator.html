üè¶ Understanding Mortgages

A mortgage is a loan used to purchase real estate, where the property serves as collateral. Monthly payments typically include principal (loan amount), interest (borrowing cost), taxes, and insurance‚Äîknown as PITI.

üìà Interest Rate Impact

Even small changes in interest rates significantly affect your total cost. A 1% difference on a $300,000 loan over 30 years can mean $60,000+ in additional interest. Always shop around for the best rates.

üí° Down Payment Tips

A 20% down payment helps you avoid Private Mortgage Insurance (PMI) and reduces monthly payments. However, many loan programs accept as little as 3-5% down for qualified buyers.

Frequently Asked Questions

Monthly mortgage payments are calculated using the formula: M = P[r(1+r)^n]/[(1+r)^n-1], where P is the principal loan amount, r is the monthly interest rate (annual rate √∑ 12), and n is the total number of payments. Our calculator also adds monthly property taxes and insurance to give you the complete picture.

A typical mortgage payment includes four components known as PITI: Principal (paying down the loan balance), Interest (the cost of borrowing money), Taxes (property taxes often escrowed monthly), and Insurance (homeowners insurance and possibly PMI if down payment is less than 20%).

A 15-year mortgage has higher monthly payments but you'll pay significantly less in total interest and build equity faster. A 30-year mortgage has lower monthly payments, giving you more flexibility, but costs more over time. Use our calculator to compare both scenarios and choose based on your budget and goals.

A common guideline is that your monthly housing payment should not exceed 28% of your gross monthly income. For example, with a $6,000 monthly income, aim for a payment under $1,680. Also consider the 36% rule‚Äîtotal debt payments (housing + other debts) should stay below 36% of gross income.
